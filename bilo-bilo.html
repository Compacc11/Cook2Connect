Ginataang Bilo-Bilo

Home Recipes

Ingredients

For the Bilo-Bilo (Glutinous Rice Balls):

  • 1 cup glutinous rice flour
  • 1/2 cup water

For the Ginataang Bilo-Bilo:

  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 cup sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 cup ripe saba bananas, sliced
  • 1 cup purple yam (ube), peeled and cubed (optional)
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 cup tapioca pearls (optional)
  • 1/2 cup jackfruit (langka), sliced (optional)
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions

Prepare the Bilo-Bilo:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine glutinous rice flour and water. Mix until a smooth dough forms.
  2. Take small portions of the dough and roll them into small balls (about the size of a marble).
  3. Set the rice balls aside.

Cook the Tapioca Pearls (if using):

  1. In a separate pot, bring water to a boil and cook the tapioca pearls until they become translucent. This usually takes about 15-20 minutes.
  2. Drain and set aside.

Making the Ginataang Bilo-Bilo:

  1. In a large pot, combine coconut milk and water. Bring to a boil over medium heat.
  2. Add the sweet potatoes and purple yam (if using) and cook until they start to soften.
  3. Add the glutinous rice balls (bilo-bilo) and cook until they float to the surface, indicating they are done.
  4. Stir in the sliced saba bananas, jackfruit (if using), and cooked tapioca pearls. Continue to cook until the bananas are tender.
  5. Add the sugar and a pinch of salt. Adjust the sweetness according to your taste.
  6. Simmer for a few more minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.

Serving:

Serve the Ginataang Bilo-Bilo warm in bowls. You can also enjoy it chilled, depending on your preference.
